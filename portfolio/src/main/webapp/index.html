Getting to know me.

My name is Michael Stone and I am a first generation college student. I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's in Computer Engineering at Columbia Univserity. My curiosity tends to be my driving force in my day to day life, and without it I would find myself very bored.

My field of study

Those who know me know I love to tinker with things. I just have a natural urge to take things apart and put them back together. For better or for worse, at times I even try "upgrading" the items I take apart which either works.... or... ends up breaking said item... Regradless there is no better field for me than engineering. I am an aspiring computer engineer. My major tends to get mixed up with computer science a lot but I assure you they are different majors. In computer engineering, I learn everything a computer scientist does; data structures, advanced algorithms, networks, AI, you name it. The key difference being computer engineers also learn the electrical engineering that comes with a computer. Now you are wondering "how do they learn everything a computer science person does with electrical engineering"? The answer is quite simple actually. We basically stuff our schedules to the brim. Me in particular I am very interested in systems engineering. Systems engineering is the magical world where hardware and software come together to make amazing things happen. Basically, the world where electricity meets code. If all goes according to plan I would like to one day pursue a masters degress in a related field in systems engineering.

Operating Systems

Class attended Spring 2020. Easily one of the hardest classes I have ever taken. The class focused on hacking the Debian Linux OS. In order to do that we had to learn in depth how diffferent parts of the OS worked together. We learned about schedulers, task-structs, synchronis and asynchronis programming, and implementing system calls just to name a few. The class had a "self exploration mind set", where we learned about concepts and theories and class and then had to dive into linux documentation on our own to find out "how to build and implement another scheduler"? Or how to add another file-system into our OS?
